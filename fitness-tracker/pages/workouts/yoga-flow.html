Yoga Sequence

Cat-Cow Pose

üê± Warm-up 1-2 minutes ‚ö° Spinal Mobility

Gentle spinal warm-up that mobilizes the spine and prepares your body for the practice. Great for releasing tension in the back and neck.

üìã Instructions

  1. Start on hands and knees in tabletop position
  2. Inhale: Arch back, lift head and tailbone (Cow)
  3. Exhale: Round spine, tuck chin to chest (Cat)
  4. Move slowly with your breath
  5. Repeat for 1-2 minutes

üí° Tips

  • Move slowly and mindfully
  • Keep shoulders over wrists, hips over knees
  • Focus on the breath-movement connection

Downward Dog

üêï Inversion 1-2 minutes ‚ö° Full Body

A foundational pose that stretches the entire back body while strengthening arms and shoulders. Great for energizing the body and calming the mind.

üìã Instructions

  1. From tabletop, tuck toes under
  2. Lift hips up and back
  3. Straighten legs as much as comfortable
  4. Press hands into mat, lengthen spine
  5. Hold for 1-2 minutes, breathing deeply

üí° Tips

  • Bend knees if hamstrings are tight
  • Focus on lengthening the spine
  • Pedal feet to warm up calves

Low Lunge

ü¶µ Hip Opener 1-2 minutes each side ‚ö° Flexibility

Opens the hip flexors and stretches the back leg while strengthening the front leg. Great for improving hip mobility and releasing tension.

üìã Instructions

  1. From downward dog, step right foot forward
  2. Lower left knee to mat
  3. Keep right knee over ankle
  4. Lift torso, engage core
  5. Hold for 1-2 minutes, then switch sides

üí° Tips

  • Use a blanket under the back knee for comfort
  • Keep the front knee tracking over the ankle
  • Lift the chest and engage the core

Warrior II

‚öîÔ∏è Standing Pose 1-2 minutes each side ‚ö° Strength & Balance

A powerful standing pose that builds strength in the legs while opening the hips and chest. Develops focus, balance, and inner strength.

üìã Instructions

  1. From low lunge, straighten back leg
  2. Turn back foot parallel to mat edge
  3. Bend front knee to 90 degrees
  4. Extend arms parallel to floor
  5. Gaze over front fingertips

üí° Tips

  • Keep both legs strong and engaged
  • Shoulders stacked over hips
  • Front knee should track over the ankle

Pigeon Pose

üïäÔ∏è Deep Hip Opener 1-2 minutes each side ‚ö° Deep Stretch

A deep hip opener that releases tension in the hips, glutes, and lower back. Excellent for improving flexibility and releasing stored emotions.

üìã Instructions

  1. From downward dog, bring right knee to right wrist
  2. Slide right foot toward left wrist
  3. Extend left leg back
  4. Square hips to front of mat
  5. Hold for 1-2 minutes, then switch sides

üí° Tips

  • Use a block under the hip for support
  • Don't force the stretch - breathe into it
  • Keep the back leg active

Seated Forward Fold

üßò Seated Pose 1-2 minutes ‚ö° Hamstring Stretch

Calms the nervous system while stretching the entire back body. Great for releasing tension and promoting relaxation.

üìã Instructions

  1. Sit with legs extended in front
  2. Inhale to lengthen spine
  3. Exhale to fold forward from hips
  4. Reach for feet, shins, or thighs
  5. Hold for 1-2 minutes, breathing deeply

üí° Tips

  • Bend knees if needed to protect the back
  • Focus on lengthening the spine
  • Use a strap around the feet if you can't reach

Supine Twist

üîÑ Spinal Twist 1-2 minutes each side ‚ö° Detoxifying

Gentle spinal twist that releases tension in the back and promotes digestion. Great for winding down the practice.

üìã Instructions

  1. Lie on back, hug knees to chest
  2. Extend arms out to sides
  3. Drop knees to the right
  4. Turn head to the left
  5. Hold for 1-2 minutes, then switch sides

üí° Tips

  • Keep both shoulders on the mat
  • Breathe deeply into the twist
  • Don't force the stretch

Savasana (Corpse Pose)

‚òÆÔ∏è Final Relaxation 3-5 minutes ‚ö° Integration

The most important pose of the practice! Allows your body to integrate all the benefits of the yoga session while promoting deep relaxation.

üìã Instructions

  1. Lie flat on your back
  2. Let feet fall open naturally
  3. Place arms at sides, palms up
  4. Close your eyes and relax completely
  5. Stay for 3-5 minutes, breathing naturally

üí° Tips

  • Use a blanket for warmth if needed
  • Let go of all effort and control
  • This is the most important pose - don't skip it!

üîÑ Pose Modifications

Adaptations for Different Levels

  • Downward Dog: Use blocks under hands or bend knees
  • Low Lunge: Use a block under the back knee
  • Warrior II: Shorten the stance or use a wall for support
  • Pigeon Pose: Use blocks under the hip or do reclined pigeon
  • Forward Fold: Bend knees or use a strap
  • Plank Transitions: Use knees-down version

üí° Props to Have Handy

  • Yoga Blocks: For support and proper alignment
  • Yoga Strap: For deeper stretches and modifications
  • Blanket: For cushioning and warmth
  • Bolster: For supported poses and relaxation
