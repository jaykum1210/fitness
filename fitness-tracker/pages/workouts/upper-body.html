Workout Overview

Focus on chest, back, shoulders, and arms with perfect push/pull balance. This routine builds foundational strength, improves posture, and creates a well-rounded upper body physique.

üí° Pro Tips

  • Warm up shoulders thoroughly with band pull-aparts and arm circles

Exercise Breakdown

Dumbbell Bench Press

üí™ Chest & Triceps 3 sets √ó 8-12 reps ‚ö° Compound

The cornerstone of chest development. Targets pectorals, anterior deltoids, and triceps while allowing for greater range of motion than barbell variations.

üìã Instructions

  1. Lie on bench with dumbbells at chest level, palms facing forward
  2. Press dumbbells up and slightly inward until arms are extended
  3. Lower with control until chest is fully stretched
  4. Keep shoulder blades retracted and core engaged
  5. Maintain slight arch in lower back

üí° Tips

  • Keep feet flat on floor for stability
  • Don't bounce weights off chest - control the descent
  • Squeeze chest at the top of each rep

One-arm Dumbbell Row

üèãÔ∏è Back & Biceps 3 sets √ó 10-12 reps/side ‚ö° Compound

Excellent for building lat width and thickness while improving unilateral strength. Targets lats, rhomboids, middle traps, and biceps.

üìã Instructions

  1. Place knee and hand on bench, other foot on floor
  2. Hold dumbbell with arm extended, core braced
  3. Pull dumbbell to lower chest/upper abdomen
  4. Squeeze shoulder blade at the top
  5. Lower with control and repeat

üí° Tips

  • Keep torso parallel to floor throughout
  • Focus on pulling with your back, not just your arm
  • Don't rotate your torso - keep it stable

Overhead Shoulder Press

üí™ Shoulders & Core 3 sets √ó 8-10 reps ‚ö° Compound

Builds powerful, well-rounded shoulders while engaging the entire core for stability. Essential for functional upper body strength.

üìã Instructions

  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, core engaged
  2. Hold dumbbells at shoulder height, palms facing forward
  3. Press weights straight up overhead
  4. Keep core tight and avoid excessive arching
  5. Lower with control to starting position

üí° Tips

  • Keep your core braced throughout the movement
  • Don't use momentum - control the weight
  • Keep elbows slightly in front of the weights

Lat Pulldown

üèãÔ∏è Back & Biceps 3 sets √ó 6-10 reps ‚ö° Compound

Builds a wide, strong back while targeting biceps. Great for developing the V-taper and improving pull-up strength.

üìã Instructions

  1. Sit at lat pulldown machine with thighs secured
  2. Grasp bar with wide, overhand grip
  3. Pull bar down to upper chest level
  4. Lean back slightly and squeeze shoulder blades
  5. Control the weight back to starting position

üí° Tips

  • Focus on pulling with your back, not just your arms
  • Keep your chest up and core engaged
  • Don't swing or use momentum

Bicep Curls

üí™ Biceps 2 sets √ó 12-15 reps ‚ö° Isolation

Direct bicep isolation exercise to build arm size and strength. Perfect finisher for your upper body workout.

üìã Instructions

  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, core engaged
  2. Hold dumbbells at sides with palms facing forward
  3. Curl weights up by flexing biceps
  4. Squeeze biceps at the top of the movement
  5. Lower with control to starting position

üí° Tips

  • Keep your elbows at your sides throughout
  • Don't swing the weights - use strict form
  • Focus on the mind-muscle connection

Tricep Rope Pushdown

üí™ Triceps 2 sets √ó 12-15 reps ‚ö° Isolation

Excellent tricep isolation exercise that targets all three heads of the tricep muscle for complete arm development.

üìã Instructions

  1. Stand at cable machine with rope attachment
  2. Keep elbows at sides, core engaged
  3. Push rope down by extending elbows
  4. Spread rope apart at bottom for extra squeeze
  5. Control the weight back to starting position

üí° Tips

  • Keep your elbows pinned to your sides
  • Don't lean forward - stay upright
  • Focus on squeezing your triceps at the bottom

üîÑ Exercise Substitutions

Alternative Exercises

  • Incline/Decline Press instead of Flat Bench Press (change emphasis)
  • Assisted Pull-ups instead of Lat Pulldown (more functional)
  • Arnold Press instead of Overhead Press (different shoulder activation)
  • Hammer Curls instead of Bicep Curls (target brachialis)
  • Overhead Tricep Extension instead of Pushdown (different angle)
  • Push-ups instead of Bench Press (bodyweight option)
